# Global Commodities 2026 — Value Investing Analysis

An institutional-style commodities research report analyzing the global commodity complex following the 2026 Iran conflict and the disruption of the Strait of Hormuz.

This project applies a **Value Investing Framework** to oil, gold, silver, copper, and uranium, evaluating each commodity against its fundamental anchor: the marginal cost of production.

Rather than forecasting short-term price movements, the report focuses on identifying where investors are paying for genuine scarcity and where they are merely paying a geopolitical premium.

---

## Research Question

How should a value investor evaluate commodities during periods of extreme geopolitical uncertainty?

Following the closure of the Strait of Hormuz and the resulting surge in commodity prices, this report examines whether market prices remain justified by underlying fundamentals or have detached from their economic anchors.

## Methodology

The analysis is built around four valuation pillars:

1. **Marginal Cost of Production**
2. **Physical Supply and Demand Balance**
3. **Real Price Mean Reversion**
4. **Market Positioning and Speculative Flows**

The central principle is simple:

> A value investor does not buy geopolitical premiums. A value investor buys assets near or below replacement cost.

---

## Key Conclusions

### Most Attractive

* Uranium

### Attractive on Corrections

* Copper

### Attractive Only After Deep Pullbacks

* Oil & Energy

### Least Attractive

* Gold
* Silver

The report concludes that uranium offers the strongest risk-reward profile because it trades close to its incentive price while benefiting from long-term structural demand driven by nuclear energy and AI-related power consumption.
